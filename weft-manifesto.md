# The Weft Manifesto

Somewhere along the way, connecting people became a business of standing between them.

The feed decides what you see. The algorithm decides who you meet. The platform learns everything about you, because knowing you is the product, and sells your attention by the millisecond to whoever bids. You cannot leave without losing everyone. That is not a community. That is a hostage arrangement with good lighting.

We remember what connection is actually made of. A friend who says *you should talk to my cousin.* A question asked at a table and carried out the door. A name vouched for by someone whose word means something. None of this ever needed a company in the middle. It needed a channel.

**So Weft is a channel, not a place.** There is nothing to visit, nothing to scroll, nothing that wants you back. It is a way of asking, the way email is a way of writing.

**We ask; we do not broadcast.** Say what you're looking for, out loud, and it travels hand to hand through the people you actually know — shedding your name at the first step, carrying only its meaning. The world doesn't need your profile. It needs your question.

**Trust is people, not points.** You will never see a score. You will see sentences: *vouched by Maya, since 2024.* A thousand fake accounts can vouch for each other; not one can forge a path through your friends.

**Nobody meets before both say yes.** Names unlock together, at the same instant, or not at all. And declining sends nothing — the protocol contains no word for *no*, so rejection can never be turned into a weapon.

**Forgetting is the default.** Asks expire. Mailboxes hold sealed envelopes for days, not dossiers for decades. Seize every server we touch and you will find ciphertext and empty shelves. What matters is kept, on purpose, by someone who cares.

**No one can buy the space between us.** The infrastructure is dumb, blind, and costs a coffee a month. There is nothing to advertise against, because there is nothing anyone in the middle can read. This is not policy. It is architecture. Promises can be broken; math has to be broken.

**Communities rule themselves.** Short rules, written by the people who live under them, agreed at the door. Govern badly and your people leave for somewhere better — the one accountability no platform has ever faced.

**And the app succeeds when you close it.** Its only measure is whether you found your people. Not minutes. Not streaks. Matches made, then silence, gladly.

The name is from weaving. The warp is the standing threads — the relationships you already have. The weft is the thread that travels across them, over and under, hand to hand, and it is the thing that turns loose strands into cloth.

The warp is already there. Come be the thread.

## The thirty-second version

*Weft is how you find your people without a platform in the middle. You say what you're looking for — out loud — and your ask travels friend to friend through your real social network, like asking around at a dinner table, without your name attached. When it finds someone who fits, you're both shown what the other is, never who, until you both say yes — and every match comes vouched by real people you can name. No feed, no ads, no company: just your community's own phones and a five-dollar encrypted mailbox it runs itself. It's slower than a feed, on purpose — and it succeeds the moment you find your people and close the app.*
